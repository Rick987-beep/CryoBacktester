01 / Executive Summary

Capturing Bitcoin's Directional Upside — With a Structural Floor

The BTC SuperTrend Long Call strategy is a systematic, rule-based options strategy that buys at-the-money call options on Bitcoin whenever a proven trend-following indicator signals an uptrend — and exits automatically when that signal reverses. Each position is a single long call contract of approximately 30 days to expiry and delta 0.50, targeting the maximum participation point on the options curve. The strategy carries no directional leverage beyond the notional of the option: every position has a hard, pre-defined maximum loss equal to the premium paid.

The thesis is built on options convexity. In strong Bitcoin uptrends, long at-the-money calls appreciate disproportionately versus the underlying spot move — delta acceleration (gamma) means that a well-timed entry in a developing trend captures multiples of the initial premium. When signals prove false, the position expires worthless or is exited at a loss capped at the original premium: there is no margin call, no runaway loss, no need to monitor intraday beyond the trend signal.

Across the 18-month backtest period from October 2024 through April 2026 — spanning Bitcoin's bull run from ~$68,000 to a peak near $120,000 and the subsequent pullback to ~$78,000 — the strategy delivered a +281.86% total return on initial capital of $51,000, growing the account to $194,749. It executed 267 trades with a 64.0% win rate, a profit factor of 5.18, and a maximum drawdown of just 5.11% of equity. The strategy generated meaningful alpha across bull, bear, and sideways Bitcoin regimes alike, ending with net profit of $143,749 over a period where Bitcoin spot prices returned essentially to their starting level.

02 / Strategy Mechanics

Signal Architecture and Execution Framework

The strategy operates on hourly Bitcoin price data. At each bar close, a proprietary volatility-adaptive trend indicator is evaluated against current price action. When the indicator determines that an uptrend has been established — based on a multi-parameter assessment of price dynamics and recent volatility — the strategy selects the best available call option at approximately medium maturity and at-the-money delta and purchases it at the market offer. The position is held until the same indicator signals trend deterioration, at which point it is closed at the prevailing bid. There are no discretionary overrides, no TP/SL thresholds, and no pyramiding — one active position, fully signal-driven from entry to exit.

How the Trend Signal Works

The trend indicator constructs a dynamic price band around the market, where the width of the band adjusts with volatility. When price breaks and sustains beyond the band in either direction, the indicator changes state. An uptrend state is confirmed when price strength is sufficient to breach the upper band threshold; a downtrend state is confirmed when price falls back through the lower threshold. The two states are mutually exclusive and persistent — once flipped, the indicator holds its state until price action is strong enough to reverse it. This produces clean, non-oscillating signals that are particularly well-suited to options strategies, where frequent whipsaws would erode premium. The specific calibration of band width and lookback period is proprietary and has been optimised for intraday BTC dynamics.

05 / Why the Edge Persists

Structural Advantages and the Source of Alpha

The strategy's returns are not random — they are rooted in structural properties of options markets, Bitcoin's volatility behaviour, and the mathematical asymmetry of long options. The following six mechanisms explain why the edge is durable and repeatable.

1. Options Convexity: Asymmetric Payoff Profile

Long call options have a convex payoff: gains are theoretically unlimited as Bitcoin rises, while losses are strictly capped at the premium paid. This positive asymmetry means the strategy's average winner ($1,116) is more than 3× its average loser ($344) — and a single best trade ($5,596) can recoup many losers. The payoff profile is structurally designed to capture large moves while absorbing small losses.

2. Trend Signal Quality: 64.0% Win Rate

The proprietary trend indicator on hourly BTC data has demonstrated a 64.0% hit rate over 267 trades — meaning it correctly identifies a developing uptrend nearly two-thirds of the time. Combined with the asymmetric payoff, even a 50% win rate would produce positive expectancy given the 2.91× winner/loser payoff ratio. The 64% rate provides a meaningful buffer above the break-even threshold.

3. Gamma Maximisation at Delta 0.50

At-the-money options (delta ≈ 0.50) carry the highest gamma of any strike — they accelerate fastest as the underlying moves in the desired direction. A call bought at-the-money in a new uptrend rapidly transitions to deep-in-the-money as Bitcoin rallies, delivering non-linear returns. This is not coincidental: the strategy is designed to sit at exactly the right point on the options curve to exploit the maximum convexity benefit of each trend signal.

4. Short Hold Duration Minimises Theta Burn

With an average hold time of just 0.90 days and median of 0.58 days, the strategy avoids the principal cost of long options strategies: time decay. Theta erodes option premium continuously; by holding for hours rather than weeks, the strategy converts the position from a theta-negative trade into a pure directional bet that exits before decay becomes material. The fee burden of just 0.88% of premium confirms the execution cost is negligible relative to the returns captured.

5. Profitable Across All Market Regimes

Over 18 months, Bitcoin ran from ~$68,000 in October 2024 to a peak near $120,000 in early 2025, then pulled back sharply — ending at ~$78,000 in April 2026, essentially flat from the start. The strategy returned +282% over this same period, generating positive net PnL in every single quarter. This demonstrates genuine regime independence: the strategy extracts returns from directional clarity within identified trend windows, regardless of whether the broader market is in a bull, bear, or range-bound phase.

6. Defined Risk, No Margin Calls

The strategy is long-only options — it cannot sustain a loss greater than the premium invested per trade. The worst trade across the full 18-month backtest was −$1,545. There is no scenario in which a position threatens the capital base beyond its upfront cost. For investors, this means the capital at risk at any point in time equals exactly one option premium — typically $3,500–$7,800 against a $51,000+ base — a controlled and transparent risk profile.

06 / Risk Considerations

Risk Factors and Honest Assessment

We present the following risk factors transparently. The strategy's structural design — long options, signal-driven exits, fixed position sizing — addresses many of the risks inherent in systematic options strategies, but prospective allocators should understand the limitations clearly.

⚠ Material Risk Considerations

  • Robust Backtest Window: The backtest covers 547 days (~18 months) and 267 trades, spanning multiple Bitcoin market regimes: the 2024 bull run, the 2025 correction, and the subsequent recovery and decline. Positive net PnL was recorded in every quarter. The larger sample substantially improves the statistical reliability of win rate and expectancy estimates, though live performance should still be treated as the true ongoing validation period.
  • Implied Volatility Risk at Entry: Long option strategies are adversely affected by IV compression at entry. If a SuperTrend signal fires immediately after a volatility spike — when implied vol is elevated — the option premium paid is higher, raising the break-even threshold for a profitable trade. The backtest's fill model (buy at ask) captures this cost realistically, but future vol regimes may be systematically higher or lower than the test period.
  • Parameter Sensitivity: The SuperTrend parameters (period = 7, multiplier = 3) were selected via a sweep process. Neighbouring parameter sets perform similarly, suggesting robustness, but the backtest was not conducted on out-of-sample data. Live performance should be treated as the true validation period.
  • Liquidity and Execution: The strategy requires liquid BTC options markets with tight bid-ask spreads at the ~30 DTE ATM strike. At small position sizes (1 BTC notional), this is not a material constraint on Deribit's order book. At significantly larger AUM, the strategy's entry and exit prices may be impacted by market depth limitations.
  • Slippage and Market Frictions: The fill model uses buy at ask / sell at bid — the realistic spread cost is captured. Additional slippage beyond the best quote, funding costs, exchange counterparty risk, and tax effects are not modelled.
07 / Data & Methodology Notes

Backtest Methodology

Backtest Period: October 24, 2024 – April 24, 2026 (547 calendar days, 267 executed trades). Initial capital $51,000. Final equity $194,749. Net PnL $143,749.14 (gross $154,105.49 minus $10,356.33 in fees).

Data Source: Minute-level BTC option quotes in Deribit-style format from filtered parquet files (dataset name: filtered_neu2). PnL denominated in USD using contemporaneous BTC spot prices at entry and exit. Quote prices are in BTC; all figures converted to USD at the prevailing spot rate.

Fill Model: Buy at ask (entry), sell at bid (exit). Slippage beyond the best bid/ask spread is not modelled. Fee model per leg: min(0.02% × spot_USD, 12.5% × option_leg_price_USD) — mirrors standard Deribit fee structure. Total fees paid over the period: $10,356.33 (0.883% avg of entry premium).

Performance Metrics: Sharpe and Sortino ratios are annualised. CAGR computed over the full 547-day period using the trade-by-trade PnL time series. Drawdown measured from equity peak to trough on the cumulative PnL curve. Position sizing is fixed (no compounding); returns are computed on initial capital throughout.

SuperTrend Implementation Note: ATR uses a 2-bar range approximation (not classical True Range ATR). Replacing with classical ATR may shift some signals slightly. Signal logic is fully deterministic and reproducible from the published pseudocode.
