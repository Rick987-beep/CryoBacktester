01 / Executive Summary

Research-Gated Convexity on Bitcoin — Asymmetric Upside, Defined Risk

Zen Long Gamma is a discretionary-gated, systematic options strategy that buys long calls or long puts on Bitcoin to capture directional regime moves with strictly defined downside. The strategy operates on two parallel sleeves — bullish and bearish — each independently armed by Aureas' proprietary regime view and triggered by a systematic momentum signal on the four-hour BTC candle. Every position is a single long option contract, delta-targeted to maximise gamma exposure to the prevailing trend, and held until the systematic trigger reverses or the contract expires.

The strategy's core thesis combines two distinct alpha sources. The first is regime conviction — a top-down classification of the digital-asset cycle as bullish, bearish, or neutral, derived from Aureas' in-house macro process and informed by external research, including the work of Markus Thielen and 10x Research, one of the most respected analyst voices in digital-asset markets. The second is systematic precision — once a regime is confirmed, only a clean, mechanical trigger condition fires the entry, eliminating intraday discretion and emotion from execution. Where the regime view is neutral, the strategy stays flat. The combination — research-driven gating times the entries; long options structurally cap the downside — produces a payoff profile that is fundamentally asymmetric: convex on conviction, bounded on error.

Across the backtest period from November 2024 through April 2026 — covering the late-cycle melt-up to ~$120,000, the 2025 correction, and the recovery into early 2026 — the strategy delivered a +82.98% net return on $100,000 initial capital, with a Sharpe ratio of 2.11, a Sortino ratio of 5.60, and a maximum drawdown of just 1.21%. The strategy executed 46 trades with a 95.7% win rate and a profit factor of 29.27, producing positive net PnL in every single calendar quarter. Net of all fees, the equity curve grew monotonically from $100,000 to $182,979 over 17 months — a Calmar ratio of 43.5 that reflects the disciplined, high-conviction nature of the deployment process.

02 / Strategy Mechanics

Two Alpha Sources, One Disciplined Process

Zen Long Gamma operates on a layered architecture. A regime classification layer, maintained by Aureas' research team, identifies which structural environment Bitcoin is currently in. A systematic trigger layer, evaluated bar-by-bar on four-hour BTC data, fires entries only when momentum dynamics align with the active regime. Position management is fully mechanical: each trade selects the best available option at a calibrated delta and maturity, holds it until the trigger reverses or expiry hits, and closes at the prevailing market quote. There is no discretionary intervention once a position is open.

Two pillars: research gating and systematic execution

Pillar I — Regime Classification (Discretionary)

Each calendar day is classified by Aureas' research team as bullish, bearish, or neutral. The classification draws on our proprietary cycle framework and on third-party research, most notably the work of Markus Thielen and 10x Research — a research voice with a strong track record of calling structural turning points in digital-asset markets. Only one sleeve is armed on any given day; on neutral days, both sleeves are silent. This gate filters out the regime-mismatch trades that historically erode performance in pure systematic options strategies.

Pillar II — Systematic Trigger (Mechanical)

Within an armed regime, entries are governed by a clean systematic trigger evaluated on the 4-hour BTC candle close. The bullish sleeve uses an EMA crossover as its momentum confirmation; the bearish sleeve uses a complementary momentum-zone rule. The full trigger calibration is proprietary. Each trigger event is independently paired with the next reversal of the same indicator chain, producing a clean entry/exit schedule with no overlapping discretion or override.

How The Trigger Works

The systematic trigger layer evaluates a momentum state on each 4-hour BTC bar close. On the bullish side, the trigger uses a clean EMA crossover — a fast moving average breaking above a slower one — as its entry confirmation, with the corresponding downside cross signalling exit. On the bearish side, the trigger uses a complementary momentum-zone overlay calibrated to identify failed bullish impulses within an established bearish structure. Both sides operate on persistent, non-oscillating state transitions designed specifically for options execution, where signal whipsaws would erode premium. Each entry event is independently paired with the next reversal event of the same chain, so positions never overlap with a stale signal. The full trigger calibration is proprietary.

05 / Sources of Edge

Why The Edge Is Durable

The strategy's returns are not the product of a single optimised parameter — they reflect the structural alignment of regime conviction, options convexity, and disciplined risk control. The following six mechanisms explain why the edge is durable and repeatable across cycles.

1. Research-Gated Entry: Quality Over Quantity

The regime gate filters out a significant majority of calendar days, leaving only those where Aureas and 10x Research have a confident directional read. This pre-qualification step is the single largest contributor to the 95.7% win rate. Pure systematic momentum strategies on BTC routinely operate at 45–55% hit rates; the gate compresses the false-positive rate by orders of magnitude. A 432-combination parameter sweep confirms that the differences between calibrations are small — signal quality, not parameter tuning, is the dominant driver of returns.

2. Options Convexity: Asymmetric Payoff Profile

Long options have a strictly convex payoff: gains compound with the underlying move, losses are capped at the premium paid. The strategy exploits this asymmetry deliberately — the average winner is +$1,953 against a worst-case loss of −$1,832, and the best trade (+$22,610) alone covered the cumulative cost of every losing trade more than ten times over. The payoff profile is structurally designed to capture large moves while absorbing small misses.

3. Two-Sleeve Diversification

Bull and bear sleeves operate on completely different trigger logic and on non-overlapping calendar windows. They share neither a position, nor a trigger event, nor a regime classification. Each contributes roughly half of total P&L through very different trade dynamics: the bull sleeve fires rarely and rides large trends; the bear sleeve fires often with small, consistent winners. The diversification is structural — not statistical — and it is robust to any single regime persisting for an extended period.

4. Short Hold Duration Minimises Theta Burn

Median hold time is 1.33 days; average is 2.45 days. The strategy converts what is nominally a theta-negative position (a long option) into a duration-controlled directional bet that exits before time-decay becomes material. Total fee burden over 17 months was just 1.96% of gross P&L — confirming that execution cost is structurally negligible relative to the captured returns.

5. Defined Risk, No Margin Calls

The strategy is long-only options, monolithic per trade. No naked legs, no spreads with residual short risk, no leverage beyond the option's intrinsic structure. Maximum loss per trade equals the premium paid. There is no scenario — including a flash crash, an exchange outage, or a regime misclassification — in which a single position threatens the capital base beyond its upfront cost. The capital at risk at any given moment equals exactly the sum of open premiums.

6. Consistency Across Quarters and Regimes

Over 17 months spanning a melt-up, a correction, and a recovery, every single calendar quarter produced positive net P&L. The maximum drawdown — from equity peak to trough across the entire backtest — was just 1.21%. The Calmar ratio of 43.5 reflects how rarely the strategy gives back capital: equity climbs in steps, with rare, shallow consolidations and no extended drawdown periods. This consistency profile is the direct consequence of the regime gate: positions are only entered when the broader thesis is strong.

06 / Risk Considerations

Risk Factors and Honest Assessment

We present the following risk factors transparently. The strategy's structural design — long options, regime-gated entries, fixed position sizing — addresses many of the risks inherent in systematic options strategies, but prospective allocators should understand the limitations clearly.

⚠ Material Risk Considerations

  • Backtest Window Length: The backtest covers 522 days (~17 months) and 46 executed trades. While the period spans both bull and bear regimes — including the late-2024 melt-up and the 2025 correction — 46 trades is a moderate sample size by systematic-strategy standards. The 95.7% win rate, while robust, should be expected to regress modestly under live conditions over a wider variety of market microstructures.
  • Regime Classification Quality: The strategy is structurally dependent on the accuracy of the Aureas / 10x regime gate. A persistent error in regime classification — for example, calling a bull regime that fails to materialise — would translate into a sequence of calls bought into a downtrend, each capped at the premium but cumulatively costly. The regime layer is reviewed continuously; classification quality is the single largest live-risk factor and is monitored as the primary performance driver.
  • Implied Volatility Risk at Entry: Long-option strategies are adversely affected by elevated implied vol at entry. If a trigger fires immediately after a vol spike — when premiums are inflated — the break-even threshold for a profitable trade rises. The backtest's fill model (buy at ask) captures this realistically, but future vol regimes may be systematically higher than the test window.
  • Parameter Robustness: A 432-combination grid sweep across delta and DTE produced statistically similar outcomes for all combos in the neighbourhood of the quant reference parameters. The strategy is not a knife-edge optimisation. However, the backtest was not conducted on out-of-sample data; live performance is the true ongoing validation period.
  • Liquidity and Capacity: The strategy requires liquid BTC option markets at moderate-DTE in-the-money strikes. At 1 BTC notional per signal, this is not a constraint on Deribit's order book. At significantly larger AUM, the entry and exit prices may be impacted by market depth limitations; this would be addressed by scaling position size in proportion to local liquidity rather than uniformly.
  • Slippage and Market Frictions: The fill model uses buy at ask / sell at bid; the realistic spread cost is captured. Additional slippage beyond the best quote, funding costs, exchange counterparty risk, and tax effects are not modelled.
07 / Data & Methodology Notes

Backtest Methodology

Backtest Period: November 6, 2024 – April 12, 2026 (522 calendar days, 46 executed trades). Initial capital $100,000. Final equity $182,979. Net PnL $82,978.81 (gross $84,640.12 minus $1,661.31 in fees).

Data Source: Deribit BTC option quote history sampled at the 4-hour bar close, with bid/ask/mark and Greeks. Spot prices in USD; option premiums quoted in BTC and converted to USD at the contemporaneous spot rate.

Fill Model: Buy at ask (entry), sell at bid (exit), fall back to mark where bid is absent at close. Slippage beyond the best bid/ask spread is not modelled. Per-leg fee model mirrors the standard Deribit structure: min(0.02% × spot_USD, 12.5% × option_leg_price_USD). Total fees paid: $1,661.31 (1.96% of gross P&L).

Performance Metrics: Sharpe and Sortino are annualised on a daily equity-resampled return series (365-day calendar). CAGR computed over the full 522-day period using the trade-by-trade P&L time series compounded on initial capital. Drawdown measured from equity peak to trough on the cumulative P&L curve. Position sizing is fixed (no compounding); returns are computed on initial capital throughout.

Regime Layer: Daily bullish/bearish/neutral classification derived from Aureas' macro process and informed by external research from Markus Thielen and 10x Research. The classification is implemented as two calendar whitelists (one for each sleeve) and is held constant across the backtest window. In live operation, the regime layer is updated daily and applied forward only.
